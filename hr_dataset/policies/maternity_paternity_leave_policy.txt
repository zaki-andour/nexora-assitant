Nexora Solutions — Maternity and Paternity Leave Policy
Document reference: HR-POL-003 | Effective: 1 January 2024 | Owner: HR Director

1. PURPOSE
Nexora Solutions is committed to supporting employees through significant life events. This policy sets out the entitlements and procedures for maternity and paternity leave.

2. MATERNITY LEAVE
2.1 Eligible employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave at full salary, followed by up to 26 additional weeks of unpaid leave if desired.
2.2 To be eligible for paid maternity leave, an employee must have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous employment with Nexora Solutions prior to the expected week of childbirth.
2.3 Employees with fewer than 12 months of service are entitled to statutory maternity pay in accordance with local employment law.
2.4 Maternity leave may begin up to 11 weeks before the expected due date. The employee must notify HR and their line manager at least 15 weeks before the expected due date.

3. PATERNITY LEAVE
3.1 Eligible employees are entitled to 4 weeks of paid paternity leave at full salary, to be taken within 56 days of the birth or adoption of the child.
3.2 Eligibility requires a minimum of 6 months of continuous employment at the time of the child's birth or adoption.
3.3 Paternity leave must be taken in a single continuous block unless otherwise agreed with the line manager and HR.

4. ADOPTION LEAVE
4.1 Employees adopting a child are entitled to the same leave provisions as maternity leave.
4.2 Only one partner in a couple may claim adoption leave equivalent to maternity leave. The other partner is entitled to paternity leave provisions.

5. SHARED PARENTAL LEAVE
Eligible employees may opt for shared parental leave in lieu of part of the maternity or adoption leave entitlement. Shared parental leave provisions are subject to applicable local legislation and must be agreed in advance with HR.

6. RETURN TO WORK
6.1 Employees returning from maternity or adoption leave are entitled to return to the same role or, where this is not reasonably practicable, to a suitable alternative role at the same grade and salary.
6.2 Employees who wish to request a change to working hours or arrangements upon return must submit a flexible working request through the standard HR process.

7. NOTIFICATION AND DOCUMENTATION
All maternity, paternity and adoption leave requests must be submitted to HR with supporting documentation (e.g. MATB1 form or adoption matching certificate) at least 8 weeks prior to the intended start of leave.

8. CONTACT
Contact hr@nexora.com or your HR Business Partner for all maternity and paternity leave queries.
