Nexora Solutions — Disciplinary Procedure
Document reference: HR-POL-008 | Effective: 1 January 2024 | Owner: HR Director

1. PURPOSE
This procedure sets out the process Nexora Solutions follows when addressing employee conduct or performance that falls below the expected standard.

2. SCOPE
This procedure applies to all permanent employees. A modified version applies to contractors as set out in their contracts.

3. TYPES OF DISCIPLINARY ISSUES
3.1 Minor misconduct: lateness, minor breaches of company policy, low-level performance issues.
3.2 Serious misconduct: repeated policy breaches, insubordination, misuse of company resources, harassment.
3.3 Gross misconduct: theft, fraud, physical violence, serious data breaches, discrimination. Gross misconduct may result in summary dismissal without notice.

4. INFORMAL STAGE
For minor misconduct, the line manager addresses the issue informally through a documented conversation. A record is kept for 6 months. If the issue persists, formal proceedings are initiated.

5. FORMAL PROCEDURE
Step 1 — Investigation: HR appoints an investigating manager to gather facts. The employee is notified of the investigation in writing.
Step 2 — Disciplinary Hearing: The employee receives at least 5 working days' notice of the hearing and may be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative.
Step 3 — Outcome: Possible outcomes include: written warning (valid 12 months), final written warning (valid 18 months), demotion, or dismissal.
Step 4 — Appeal: The employee may appeal within 10 working days of receiving the outcome.
